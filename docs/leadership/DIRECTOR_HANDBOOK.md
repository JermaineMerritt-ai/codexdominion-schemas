## 8. The Director's Crisis Protocols

Directors handle crises with **calm and clarity**.

### Youth Issues

**Types:**
- Safety concerns (bullying, abuse, mental health)
- Conflict between youth
- Youth dropping out or disengaging
- Inappropriate behavior in circles

**Protocol:**
1. **Immediate** â€” Ensure youth safety (remove from danger, contact authorities if needed)
2. **Escalate** â€” Notify Country Director within 24 hours
3. **Coordinate** â€” Work with school administration, parents, counselors
4. **Document** â€” Record incident, actions taken, outcomes
5. **Follow up** â€” Check on youth, captain, and circle weekly for 4 weeks
6. **Learn** â€” Update protocols to prevent future incidents

**Director's Role:**
- Act quickly and decisively
- Follow safety protocol (youth safety > program continuity)
- Support captain emotionally (crisis affects them too)
- Communicate transparently with stakeholders (school, parents, ministry)

---

### School Issues

**Types:**
- Principal requests circles stop
- School cancels space/time for circles
- Teacher/administrator conflict
- School policy change affecting program

**Protocol:**
1. **Meet principal** â€” Within 48 hours (face-to-face, Director attends)
2. **Listen** â€” Understand their concern fully (don't defend immediately)
3. **Assess** â€” Is this solvable or terminal?
4. **Respond** â€” If solvable, propose solution; if terminal, gracefully exit
5. **Deploy ambassador** â€” To stabilize or transition
6. **Document** â€” Record issue, resolution, and learnings

**Director's Role:**
- Represent Dominion with professionalism
- Prioritize school relationship over program continuation (if needed)
- Don't pressure principals (builds resentment)
- Learn from the issue (improve future school partnerships)

---

### Ambassador Issues

**Types:**
- Ambassador underperforming (low activation rate, poor quality)
- Ambassador burnout
- Ambassador cultural misalignment
- Ambassador misconduct (ethical violations)

**Protocol:**
1. **Retrain** â€” If skill gap, provide coaching and resources
2. **Reassign** â€” If capacity issue, reduce school load or shift to support role
3. **Escalate** â€” If misconduct or repeated failure, involve Country Director
4. **Terminate** â€” If necessary (with Country Director approval)

**Retraining Process:**
- 4-week intensive coaching (weekly 1:1s)
- Co-facilitate school visits (observe and provide feedback)
- Clear performance expectations with measurable targets
- Weekly progress reviews
- Decision point at Week 4 (continue, reassign, or terminate)

**Director's Role:**
- Coach first, terminate last
- Document performance issues clearly
- Provide fair warning and improvement opportunity
- Make hard decisions when needed (protect program quality)

**Protocol:**

**Director's Role:**

- Respect local context (adapt delivery, not message)

- Escalate to Council if conflict is systemic (requires Dominion-wide solution)

**Director's Role:**
