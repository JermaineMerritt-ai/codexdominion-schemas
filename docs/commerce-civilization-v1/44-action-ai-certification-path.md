### **What Users Learn**

#### **1. System Fundamentals**

**What Action AI Is:**
- Structured AI workforce designed for execution, not decision-making
- 6 specialized roles with automatic routing
- Command-based interaction system
- Quality-assured outputs through 8-step checklist

**What Action AI Can Do:**
- Content creation (emails, descriptions, scripts)
- Structuring & organization (checklists, outlines, templates)
- Optimization (rewriting, shortening, improving clarity)
- Analysis (summarizing, extracting key points, comparing)
- Execution (assembling final versions, preparing deliverables)

**What Action AI Cannot Do:**
- Make strategic decisions or recommendations
- Interpret sensitive content (personal, cultural, political)
- Provide licensed professional advice (legal, medical, financial)
- Add complexity without permission
- Use emotional language unless requested

**Reference:** Manual 35 (Master Handbook), Manual 42 (System Overview Deck)

#### **2. Basic Command Vocabulary**

**Essential Action Verbs:**
- **Draft** ‚Äì First version, needs refinement
- **Write** ‚Äì Polished version, ready to use
- **Summarize** ‚Äì Condense to essentials
- **Organize** ‚Äì Restructure into specific format
- **Rewrite** ‚Äì Improve existing content
- **Create** ‚Äì Build structured deliverable

**Command Formula:**  
Action Verb + What You Want + Key Details

**Examples:**
- "Draft a 150-word product description."
- "Summarize this report in 5 bullet points."
- "Organize this into numbered steps."

**Reference:** Manual 38 (User Command Glossary)

#### **3. Clear Instruction Principles**

**Principle 1: Be Specific**
- ‚ùå "Help me with an email."
- ‚úÖ "Write a 100-word professional email announcing our product launch."

**Principle 2: Set Constraints**
- ‚ùå "Write a product description."
- ‚úÖ "Write a 150-word product description for busy professionals."

**Principle 3: Use Action Verbs**
- ‚ùå "I need something for social media."
- ‚úÖ "Draft 3 Instagram captions for our new product (50 words each)."

**Reference:** Manual 39 (Team Training Module)

#### **4. Simple Revision Requests**

**When Output Needs Adjustment:**

**Too Long:**  
"Make this more concise." or "Shorten to 100 words."

**Wrong Tone:**  
"Rewrite in a professional tone." or "Rewrite in a friendly tone."

**Wrong Focus:**  
"Focus only on customer benefits." or "Remove technical details."

**Reference:** Manual 37 (Troubleshooting Guide)

---

#### **5. Avoiding Ambiguity**

**Common Sources of Ambiguity:**
- Missing length constraints
- Unspecified tone
- Unclear audience
- Vague action verbs ("help," "fix," "improve")
- Multiple requests in one command

**Clarity Checklist:**
- ‚ñ° Action verb specified?
- ‚ñ° What I want clearly stated?
- ‚ñ° Length/format defined?
- ‚ñ° Tone indicated (if relevant)?
- ‚ñ° Single request per command?

---

#### **6. Restricted Topics**

**What to Avoid:**
- Strategic business decisions ("Should we expand to Europe?")
- Personal content interpretation ("Is this customer justified?")
- Licensed advice requests ("Is this legally binding?")
- Sensitive cultural/political topics without clear framing

**When Restricted:**  
AI will escalate. Reframe request within allowed scope.

**Reference:** Manual 25 (Safety & Compliance Addendum)

### **What Users Learn**

#### **1. Breaking Tasks Into Steps**

**Layer-Based Building:**
- Layer 1: Draft outline/structure
- Layer 2: Write detailed content
- Layer 3: Refine tone and clarity
- Layer 4: Finalize for delivery

**Why Layering Works:**
- Prevents overwhelming complexity
- Allows focused refinement at each stage
- Easier to course-correct early
- Higher quality final output

**Example Workflow:**
```
Task: Create product landing page copy

Step 1: "Draft an outline for a product landing page."
Step 2: "Write the introduction section using this outline."
Step 3: "Write the features section."
Step 4: "Rewrite introduction to be more direct."
Step 5: "Assemble sections into final page copy."
```

**Reference:** Manual 29 (Workflow Map), Manual 43 (System Playbook Scenario 1)

**Assembly Workflows:**

#### **5. Using the Troubleshooting Guide**

**Common Issues & Quick Fixes:**

**Issue:** Output too long  
**Fix:** "Make this more concise" or "Shorten to [X] words"

**Issue:** Wrong tone  
**Fix:** "Rewrite in a [professional/casual/direct] tone"

**Issue:** Misunderstood request  
**Fix:** "Rewrite this to match my original request"

**Issue:** AI asking too many questions  
**Fix:** "You have enough information‚Äîproceed"

**Issue:** Output drifted from scope  
**Fix:** "Focus only on [X]. Remove [Y]."

**Issue:** Stuck in revision loop  
**Fix:** "Let's restart. Here's what I need: [clear request]"

**Reference:** Manual 37 (Troubleshooting Guide), Manual 43 (Playbook Scenario 7)

#### **6. Resetting or Restarting Tasks**

**When to Reset:**
- After 3 revision attempts with no improvement
- When output has drifted too far from original goal
- When initial instructions were unclear

**How to Reset:**
- "Let's restart."
- "Start fresh with this request: [clear instructions]"
- "Ignore previous output. Here's what I need: [restate goal]"

**Reset vs Revision:**
- **Revision:** Small adjustments to existing output
- **Reset:** Complete restart with new clarity

**Reference:** Manual 30 (Lifecycle Model), Manual 37 (Troubleshooting Guide)

#### **3. Auditing Outputs Using Performance Metrics**

**6-Category Performance Scoring (1-5 Scale):**

**1. Accuracy**  
Are all facts, data, and details correct?  
1 = Major errors | 5 = Completely accurate

**2. Clarity**  
Is the output easy to understand?  
1 = Confusing | 5 = Perfectly clear

**4. Consistency**  
Does it match established standards and tone?  
1 = Inconsistent | 5 = Perfectly aligned

**5. Adherence**

1 = Boundary violations | 5 = Full compliance

**6. Completeness**

#### **4. Identifying & Correcting Drift**

**Types of Drift:**

**Tone Drift:**  
Outputs shift from professional to overly casual or formal

**Scope Drift:**  
Outputs include content beyond original request

**Quality Drift:**  
Standards slip, outputs require more revisions

**Boundary Drift:**  
Requests approach or cross restricted domains

**Detection Methods:**
- Weekly audit of 10 outputs
- Monthly team check-ins
- Quarterly full system review
- Performance metrics tracking

**Correction Protocols:**

**Minor Drift:**  
Team reminder of specific manual section (e.g., Manual 27 for tone)

**Moderate Drift:**  
Retraining session using relevant manual

**Severe Drift:**  
Full team reorientation using Manual 36 (Onboarding Guide)

**Reference:** Manual 33 (Maintenance & Update Schedule), Manual 40 (Leadership Guide)

**3. Enforce Boundaries**
