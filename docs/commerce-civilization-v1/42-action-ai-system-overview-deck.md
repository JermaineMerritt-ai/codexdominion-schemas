## **SLIDE 8 — HOW TASKS FLOW**

### **The 6-Stage Lifecycle**

Every task follows the same predictable sequence:

---

**Stage 1: Request**  
User provides clear instruction with action verb + details

**Stage 2: Processing**  
System interprets request, routes to appropriate role, prepares execution

**Stage 3: Output**  
AI delivers clean, structured result matching request specifications

**Stage 4: Review**  
Human evaluates output against requirements and quality standards

**Stage 5: Revision (If Needed)**  
User requests specific changes using clear commands

**Stage 6: Completion**  
Task marked complete, output approved for use

---

**Lifecycle Flowchart:**
```
Request → Processing → Output → Review
                                   ↓
                         ┌─────────┴─────────┐
                         ↓                   ↓
                      Approve            Revise
                         ↓                   ↓
                    Completion         Processing
                                           ↓
                                        Output
```

**Average Time:**  
Simple tasks: 1-2 minutes | Complex tasks: 5-15 minutes

## **SLIDE 9 — WHAT ACTION AI CAN DO**

### **The 5 Core Task Types**

Action AI supports five categories of work:

---

**1. Content Creation**  
Drafting text, outlines, scripts, descriptions, emails, social posts

**Examples:**
- Write a welcome email for new customers
- Draft a blog post about remote work

**2. Structuring & Organization**  
Creating checklists, organizing folders, building templates, summarizing information

**Examples:**
- Create a checklist for launching a product
- Organize these customer requests by priority

**3. Optimization**  
Rewriting for clarity, adjusting tone, shortening content, improving readability

**Examples:**
- Rewrite this to be more professional
- Make this email more concise
- Improve the readability of this report

**4. Analysis**  
Comparing options, extracting key points, identifying gaps, pulling insights

**Examples:**
- Compare these two pricing models
- Extract the key points from this meeting transcript
- Identify what's missing from this proposal

**5. Execution**  
Assembling final versions, combining sections, preparing deliverables

**Examples:**
- Assemble this into a final document
- Combine these three sections into one page
- Prepare a clean version for distribution

---

**Coverage:** If your request fits one of these 5 categories, the system can handle it.

## **SLIDE 10 — WHAT ACTION AI CANNOT DO**

### **The 5 Core Boundaries**

To maintain safety and predictability, Action AI **must not:**

---

**1. Make Strategic Decisions**  
Cannot choose between options, set strategy, or make business judgments

❌ "Should we expand to Europe or Asia?"  
✅ "Compare pros and cons of expanding to Europe vs. Asia."

**2. Interpret Sensitive Content**  
Cannot make judgments about personal, cultural, or political content

❌ "Is this customer complaint justified?"  
✅ "Summarize what the customer said."

**3. Provide Licensed Professional Advice**  
Cannot give legal, medical, or financial advice

❌ "Is this contract legally binding?"  
✅ "Summarize the key terms in this contract."

**4. Escalate Complexity Without Permission**  
Cannot add features, sections, or complexity unless explicitly requested

❌ Adding pricing section to product description without being asked  
✅ Delivers exactly what was requested, nothing more

**5. Use Emotional or Symbolic Language (Unless Requested)**  
Defaults to clear, professional language

❌ "Your majestic digital empire awaits…"  
✅ "Your dashboard is ready."

---

**Boundary Principle:**  
Boundaries keep the system stable, predictable, and safe.

**Enforcement:** Manual 25 (Safety & Compliance) defines all 11 restricted domains.

## **SLIDE 11 — HOW TO WORK WITH THE SYSTEM**

### **Command Language**

Use **clear, direct commands** for predictable results:

---

**Essential Commands:**

**Creation:**  
- **Draft…** → First version (needs refinement)
- **Write…** → Polished version (ready to use)

**Organization:**  
- **Summarize…** → Condense to essentials

**Optimization:**  
- **Rewrite…** → Improve existing content
- **Make more concise** → Reduce length

**Analysis:**  
- **Compare…** → Side-by-side evaluation

**Execution:**  
- **Assemble…** → Combine into final version

---

**Command Formula:**  
**Action Verb** + **What You Want** + **Key Details**

**Example:**  
"Draft a 150-word product description for yoga mats aimed at beginners."

**Why Commands Work:**  
Tested vocabulary eliminates ambiguity and produces consistent results.

## **SLIDE 13 — TROUBLESHOOTING**

### **Quick Fixes for Common Issues**

If something feels off, use **simple commands** to fix it:

---

**Issue: Output Has Wrong Focus**  
**Fix:** "Rewrite this to match the original request."

**Issue: Output Too Long**  
**Fix:** "Make this more concise." or "Shorten to 150 words."

**Issue: Wrong Tone**  
**Fix:** "Rewrite in a professional tone."

**Issue: AI Asking Too Many Questions**  
**Fix:** "You have enough information — proceed."

**Issue: AI Misinterpreted Request**  
**Fix:** "Focus only on [X]. Ignore [Y]."

**Issue: Stuck in Revision Loop**  
**Fix:** "Let's restart. Here's what I need: [clear request]."

---

**Troubleshooting Principle:**  
Most issues resolve in 2-5 minutes with specific commands.

**Troubleshooting Rate:**  
95% of issues resolved via 8-step checklist (Manual 37).

**When to Reset:**  
After 3 revision cycles with no improvement.
