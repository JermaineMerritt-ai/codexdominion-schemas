### Component 2: Inter-World Voting System (IWVS)

**Purpose:** Democratic decision-making with multiple thresholds

**Vote Thresholds:**
- **51% Simple Majority** - Routine decisions, resource allocation
- **66% Supermajority** - Structural changes, new world creation, cultural patterns

**Voting Mechanics:**
- Weighted votes (based on representative voting power)
- Three options: Approve, Reject, Abstain
- Abstentions don't count toward threshold
- Transparent reasoning required

## üéì Lessons & Best Practices

### Governance Design:
1. **Balance representation** - 3 reps per world (creative, continuity, operations)
2. **Weight by merit** - Quality and collaboration matter
3. **Cap influence** - Prevent single-world dominance
4. **Multiple thresholds** - Match decision importance to threshold
5. **Sovereign escalation** - Critical issues go to top authority

### Dispute Resolution:
1. **Structured process** - File, argue, mediate, vote
2. **Fair compromise** - Find middle ground when possible
3. **Transparent reasoning** - All arguments documented
4. **Time limits** - Don't let disputes drag on
5. **Escalation pathway** - Clear route to sovereign

### Alignment Monitoring:
1. **Three pillars** - Identity, autonomy, harmony
2. **Regular assessment** - Monitor all worlds continuously
3. **Early detection** - Catch drift before crisis
4. **Actionable recommendations** - Tell worlds what to improve
5. **Positive reinforcement** - Celebrate excellent alignment
